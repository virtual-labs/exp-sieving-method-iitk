<b>Materials Required:</b><br>
● Set of standard sieves (e.g., 10 to 325 mesh)<br>
● Granular sample (e.g., sand, starch, or pharmaceutical powder)<br>
● Weighing balance (accuracy ±0.01 g)<br>
● Mechanical sieve shaker / manual setup<br>
● Brush (for cleaning sieves)<br>
● Collection pan<br>
● Stopwatch or timer<br>
● Calculator / spreadsheet for analysis<br>

<b>Procedure:</b><br>
● Arrange the sieves in descending order (coarsest on top, finest at bottom).<br>
● Weigh approximately 100 g of the dry sample.<br>
● Place a collection pan under the bottom sieve.<br>
● Transfer the sample to the top sieve and secure the lid.<br>
● Shake manually or mechanically for 10–15 minutes.<br>
● Carefully remove and weigh the material retained on each sieve.<br>
● Record the weights and calculate:<br>
   • Percentage retained = (Weight retained / Total weight) × 100<br>
   • Cumulative % passing = 100 – Cumulative % retained<br>
● Plot a graph of log particle size (X-axis) vs. % finer (Y-axis).<br>
● Determine D₁₀, D₅₀, and D₉₀ from the curve.<br>

<b>Precautions:</b><br>
● Ensure sieves are clean and dry before use.<br>
● Avoid overloading sieves.<br>
● Maintain consistent shaking duration and motion.<br>
● Handle sieves gently to prevent mesh damage.<br>
